Defibrillator: Operation, Functional & Periodic Performance Tests (LIFEPAK 15)

This lab develops competence in safe operation and structured performance verification of a modern biphasic defibrillator/monitor (LIFEPAK 15) using a dedicated defibrillator / pacer analyzer (Fluke Impulse 7000DP) and an oscilloscope. You will execute selected procedures from the Performance Inspection Procedure (PIP) and Service Manual: Delivered Energy, Charge Time, Sync, Pacer Characteristics, and Waveform Output. All energy discharges are directed into the analyzer (never a person). Use low energy (e.g., 5 J) while familiarizing yourself with controls to preserve battery capacity.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify user interface elements, accessories, and safety interlocks of LIFEPAK 15.
  • Explain principles of transthoracic defibrillation and biphasic truncated exponential (BTE) waveforms.
  • Perform analyzer‑based Delivered Energy, Charge Time, Sync, and Pacer tests according to PIP.
  • Capture and interpret a defibrillation waveform on an oscilloscope; extract phase durations, peak voltage, and estimate delivered energy.
  • Assess pacer output current, pulse width, and rate accuracy versus specifications.
  • Document findings, compare to manufacturer tolerances, and determine pass/fail.

Safety & Professional Conduct

  • Clear the area: Ensure no person (including yourself) is in contact with patient cables, electrodes, or analyzer plates when the device is charged. Verbally announce “Charging” and “Clear – shocking”.
  • All discharges go into the analyzer test load. Do not deliver energy to any person or improvised load.
  • Use lowest practical energies while learning controls; only raise energy for specified tests.
  • Return the defibrillator to the charger after lab so the next group has a fully charged unit.
  • Leakage current tests are not performed here (covered in a separate lab); acknowledge they are part of a full safety inspection.
  • Handle paddles and accessories carefully; avoid fluid ingress; inspect for damage before testing.

Background Summary

Defibrillation physiology: A high‑energy, brief biphasic current pulse depolarizes a critical mass of myocardial cells, terminating fibrillation and allowing the sinus node to reassert control. Success probability correlates with delivered current density through the myocardium, which depends on patient transthoracic impedance (Z).

Biphasic truncated exponential (BTE) waveform: Two sequential exponential phases of opposite polarity, each truncated at a controlled time or voltage. Energy is preset by charging a capacitor to voltage V, then discharging through the patient (or test load). Ideal energy estimate: J ≈ 0.5 · C · V2 minus losses; actual delivered energy varies with Z due to current limiting and waveform truncation control.

Charge time: Interval from charge initiation to “ready” indication; must be within spec (increases with selected Joules and battery depletion).

Sync (cardioversion): Device detects R‑waves; shock is delivered within a defined window after the R to avoid T‑wave (vulnerable period). Pass criteria: marker alignment and timing tolerance (consult manual).

Pacer function: LIFEPAK 15 can deliver transcutaneous pacing pulses. Key parameters: pacing current (mA), pulse width (ms), and rate (bpm). Accuracy and consistency across impedance loads must meet tolerance.

Pre‑Procedure Checklist

  1. Visually inspect device, cables, paddles / QUICK-COMBO pads, and analyzer connections; note any defects.
  2. Verify analyzer self‑test passed and correct test load impedance selections are available (e.g., 25, 50, 75, 100 Ω).
  3. Confirm oscilloscope probe / attenuation suits expected peak voltage (consult analyzer output specs—never exceed probe rating).
  4. Review manufacturer tolerances for each planned test (Delivered Energy percent or Joule tolerance, charge time max, sync timing window, pacer current & rate ±% ranges).
  5. Ensure adequate battery (≥50%) or connect to mains charger (record mode used).
  6. Prepare data sheet with columns for: Test, Setting, Load (Ω), Measured, Spec, Pass/Fail, Notes.

Procedure

  1. Familiarization (Low Energy): Set 5 J, select paddles or pads mode on analyzer; charge and discharge into analyzer to observe workflow. Practice CLEAR verbalization.
  2. Delivered Energy Test: For a set of energies (e.g., 50 J, 100 J, 200 J) and impedance loads (e.g., 50 Ω and 100 Ω) on Impulse 7000DP: charge, deliver shock, record selected vs analyzer “delivered energy” reading. Repeat if outside tolerance to exclude operator error. Compare percentage error: error % = (Delivered - Selected)/Selected · 100.
  3. Charge Time Test: Select a high energy (e.g., 200 J). Start charge while timing (stopwatch or analyzer timer). Record charge time. If spec requires AC vs battery comparison note mode used.
  4. Sync Test: Enable SYNC. Provide analyzer simulated ECG with stable R‑waves (set heart rate ~80 bpm). Observe displayed markers; deliver synchronized shock. Record timing difference (ms) between R marker and shock marker (analyzer provides measurement). Verify within allowed window.
  5. Pacer Characteristics Test: Switch to pacing mode. Select rate (e.g., 70 bpm), current (e.g., 60 mA), and default pulse width. Analyzer displays measured rate, current, and pulse width; vary current (e.g., 30–90 mA) and load impedance (e.g., 50 & 100 Ω) to observe regulation. Record deviations.
  6. Waveform Output Test (Oscilloscope): Connect oscilloscope across analyzer’s high‑level waveform output (or differential output as instructed). Select a mid‑range energy (e.g., 100 J) at 50 Ω load. Charge and capture waveform. Measure: Phase 1 duration (t1), peak V1, Phase 2 duration (t2), peak V2, total delivered energy (compare to analyzer reading), and any overshoot or ringing. (If analyzer supplies an isolated BNC scaled output, apply correct scale factor.)
  7. Data Integrity: Complete pass/fail evaluation against specs. Highlight any out‑of‑tolerance results; repeat once to confirm. If still failing, note possible causes (battery low, connection resistance, incorrect load selection) and flag for instructor.
  8. Restore & Charge: Power down analyzer, set defibrillator on charger, tidy leads, archive data sheet.

Measurements & Analysis

  1. Delivered Energy Error: Tabulate selected vs delivered vs percent error. Confirm all within manufacturer tolerance (e.g., ±(15% or ±4 J)—verify actual spec from documentation).
  2. Impedance Dependence: Plot delivered energy vs impedance for a fixed selected Joule setting; comment on compensation behavior.
  3. Charge Time: Compare measured time to spec. Discuss factors that increase charge time (battery age, energy level, ambient temperature).
  4. Sync Timing: Evaluate R‑to‑shock delay; state compliance with window (e.g., ±10 ms of specified target). Discuss risk if synchronization mis‑fires on T‑wave.
  5. Pacer Accuracy: Compute % deviation for rate and current: dev% = (Measured − Set) / Set · 100. Note if current drops at higher impedance.
  6. Waveform Shape: Compare measured phase durations and peak voltages to typical BTE profile. If available, integrate waveform (numerical approximation) to estimate Joules and compare to analyzer value.

Documentation (Running Protocol)

  • Date/time, device ID, software version (if displayed), battery %.
  • Environmental notes (approx. room temp, mains / battery mode).
  • For each test: settings, load, measured values, spec, pass/fail, operator initials.
  • Waveform screenshot (oscilloscope hardcopy or photo) annotated with t1, t2, V1, V2.
  • Any anomalies and corrective actions (re-tested, connector reseated, etc.).

Reflection & Questions

  1. Which parameter was closest to its tolerance limit? Possible physiological or technical impact if it drifts further?
  2. How does patient impedance influence delivered energy in a BTE system vs a monophasic system?
  3. Why is synchronization critical for cardioversion but not for ventricular fibrillation?
  4. What factors could prolong charge time besides battery depletion?
  5. How would you expand this protocol to include electrical safety (leakage) testing?

Common Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to set analyzer load impedance before discharge.
  • Not re‑arming SYNC between shocks (some devices require re‑enable).
  • Incorrect oscilloscope scaling leading to misread peak voltage.
  • Recording analyzer selected instead of delivered value.
  • Allowing electrodes/cables to contact conductive surfaces while charged.

Checklist (Sign Off)

  • Controls & accessories familiarization done at low energy.
  • Delivered Energy test (≥2 energies × ≥2 impedances) complete & within spec.
  • Charge Time measured & within spec.
  • Sync timing verified & documented.
  • Pacer current, rate, pulse width measured across impedances.
  • Waveform captured & parameters extracted.
  • All data logged; device returned to charger.
