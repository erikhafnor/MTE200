Electrocardiogram (ECG) Recording

In this lab you will learn how to perform a safe, high‑quality 12‑lead ECG acquisition, interpret key waveform components, and reflect on sources of error and artifact. Follow each section carefully; safety and patient comfort come first.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the physiological basis of the ECG (depolarization / repolarization).
  • Identify the standard 12 leads and correct electrode placement landmarks.
  • Record a clean ECG minimizing motion, muscle, baseline wander, and mains interference.
  • Measure heart rate, PR interval, QRS duration, QT/QTc, and electrical axis.
  • Recognize common artifacts and propose mitigation strategies.
  • Document findings using standardized report elements.

Workflow Overview

  1. Prep
    Confirm calibration, gather electrodes, explain procedure.
  2. Skin
    Clean & (lightly) abrade for low impedance.
  3. Placement
    Apply limb then precordial leads (V1→V6 sequence).
  4. Acquire
    Subject relaxed; capture ≥10 s 12‑lead strip.
  5. Measure
    Intervals (PR, QRS, QT/QTc) & axis.
  6. Artifact Review
    Identify & mitigate noise sources; repeat if needed.
  7. Report
    Document values, rhythm, quality notes.

Safety & Professional Conduct

  • Obtain informed consent; explain procedure and what the subject will feel.
  • Ensure privacy and appropriate draping when placing limb and precordial electrodes.
  • Do not place electrodes over irritated skin or open wounds.
  • Disinfect reusable leads/electrodes according to local infection control policy.
  • Remove power sources from portable devices not needed during recording to reduce noise.

Equipment

  • ECG acquisition system (12‑lead) with calibrated paper or digital output.
  • Electrodes (pre-gelled disposable recommended) and lead wires.
  • Skin prep materials: alcohol swabs, abrasive pad (if required).
  • Measuring calipers or on‑screen digital measurement tools.
  • Ruler and calculator (or software) for interval and axis calculations.

Background Summary

The ECG represents summed cardiac electrical activity projected onto frontal and horizontal planes. Accurate interpretation depends on correct electrode positioning and awareness of factors that distort the signal (contact impedance, motion, filtering).

Pre‑Procedure Checklist

  1. Verify device calibration and paper speed (25 mm/s) & amplitude (10 mm/mV).
  2. Confirm patient identity (if applicable) and absence of contraindications.
  3. Inspect skin; gently abrade and cleanse to reduce impedance (<5 kΩ ideal).
  4. Lay out electrodes in anatomical placement order to streamline workflow.

Electrode Placement Landmarks

  • RA / LA / RL / LL: distal forearms and lower legs (or proximal if tremor).
  • V1: 4th intercostal space (ICS), right sternal border.
  • V2: 4th ICS, left sternal border.
  • V3: Midway between V2 and V4.
  • V4: 5th ICS, mid‑clavicular line.
  • V5: Anterior axillary line, level with V4.
  • V6: Midaxillary line, level with V4.

Procedure

  1. Prepare skin and apply limb electrodes symmetrically.
  2. Palpate sternal angle to locate 2nd rib, count down to 4th ICS; place V1 & V2.
  3. Place remaining precordial leads V4 then V3 (between V2/V4), followed by V5 and V6 on same horizontal level as V4.
  4. Ask subject to relax, breathe normally, minimize muscle tension; support arms.
  5. Initiate recording; obtain at least 10 seconds of all 12 leads.
  6. Repeat a lead if artifact (baseline wander >5 mm, 50/60 Hz interference, muscle noise) obscures interpretation.

Measurements & Analysis

  1. Determine rhythm regularity (R‑R variability) and heart rate (rate = 300 / large boxes or count beats in 10 s * 6).
  2. Measure PR interval (start P to start QRS), normal 120–200 ms.
  3. Measure QRS duration (narrow <120 ms in adults).
  4. Measure QT and correct (Bazett or Fridericia).
  5. Estimate electrical axis (lead I vs aVF method or vector quadrant).

Documentation

  • Date/time, subject ID (or simulated), operator initials.
  • Paper speed & gain confirmation.
  • Heart rate, PR, QRS, QT/QTc, axis.
  • Rhythm description and any notable waveform abnormalities.
  • Artifact notes and corrective actions taken.

Reflection & Questions

  1. Which artifact was most challenging to eliminate and why?
  2. How does electrode misplacement (e.g., V1 one space too high) affect morphology?
  3. Explain why QT correction is necessary at high or low heart rates.
  4. Suggest improvements to the acquisition protocol to further reduce noise.

Extension (Optional)

Record an additional ECG after light exercise. Compare rate, PR, QRS, and QT/QTc; discuss autonomic influences observed.

Common Pitfalls

  • Skipping skin abrasion leading to high impedance and noise.
  • Incorrect V1/V2 intercostal space selection.
  • Forgetting to verify calibration before interpretation.
  • Over-filtering which can distort ST segments.

Checklist (Sign Off)

  • All 12 leads captured clearly.
  • Intervals measured & documented.
  • Axis estimated.
  • Artifacts identified & mitigated.
  • Reflection questions answered.
